Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 17, 2026

The short version: We collect only what we need to run Rally. We don't sell your data. We don't track you across the internet. Your care circle information stays private.

Who We Are

Rally is operated by Kyudo Growth Pty Ltd (ABN 92 632 421 875), based in Queensland, Australia. When we say "Rally", "we", or "us", we mean Kyudo Growth Pty Ltd.

We are committed to handling your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

What We Collect

Data Why We Need It Lawful Basis
Email address Sign-in, account verification, notifications Consent; necessary for service delivery
Display name Shown to your circle so they know who you are Consent; necessary for service delivery
Circle and task data Core app functionality Necessary for service delivery
Device push token Send you task reminders and updates Consent
Device install ID Recognise your device for seamless access Legitimate interest
Aggregate usage data Understand how Rally is used so we can improve it (no individual identification) Legitimate interest

What We Don't Collect

  • Your location (unless you manually enter it for a task)
  • Your contacts or phone number
  • Photos or media from your device
  • Browsing history or app usage patterns that identify you
  • Payment or credit card information (all payments are handled by Apple and Google)
  • Health or medical information (Rally coordinates tasks, not care itself)

How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:

  • Run the Rally app and sync your circles
  • Send you task reminders you have opted into
  • Notify you when helpers claim or complete tasks
  • Improve Rally based on aggregate, anonymous usage patterns
  • Respond to support requests
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not use your data for profiling, automated decision-making, or targeted advertising.

Who Sees Your Data

  • Your circle members see your display name and tasks you interact with
  • Cloud infrastructure providers (Supabase for database hosting; Vercel for web hosting) process your data under strict data processing agreements
  • Transactional email providers deliver notifications on our behalf under data processing agreements
  • Nobody else — we do not sell, rent, or share your data with advertisers, data brokers, or any other third parties

Where We Store Data

Your data is stored on Supabase (database) and Vercel (web hosting), with servers located in the United States and Australia.

Where your data is transferred outside Australia, we take reasonable steps to ensure the overseas recipient handles your information consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles, in accordance with APP 8. Our service providers are bound by data processing agreements that require them to protect your information to standards equivalent to or exceeding Australian requirements.

Data Retention

  • Active accounts: Data is retained while your account is active and for the period necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy
  • Deleted accounts: Personal data is deleted within 30 days of account deletion. Anonymised, aggregate data may be retained for product improvement
  • Inactive circles: Archived after 12 months of inactivity. You will be notified before archival

Data Security

We use industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest, access controls limiting who within our organisation can view personal data, and regular review of our security practices.

No system is completely secure. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at privacy@rally-around.us.

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm to you, we will notify affected users as soon as practicable and no later than 30 days after becoming aware of the breach. Where required, we will also notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act 1988.

Your Rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information that we hold
  • Correct your personal information if it is inaccurate, incomplete, or out of date
  • Delete your account and have your personal data removed from our systems
  • Export your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent for optional data processing (such as push notifications) at any time
  • Complain to the OAIC if you believe we have breached your privacy

To exercise these rights, email privacy@rally-around.us. We will respond within 30 days.

If you are located in the European Union or the United Kingdom, you may also have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or UK GDPR, including the right to data portability and the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

Cookies and Tracking

The Rally website uses essential cookies only, for functionality such as keeping you signed in. We do not use advertising trackers, social media pixels, or analytics tools that identify individual users.

Children's Privacy

Rally is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@rally-around.us and we will delete that information promptly.

Users aged 13 to 17 may use Rally with parental or guardian consent.

Google Play and Apple App Store

Rally is distributed through the Google Play Store and the Apple App Store. The data we collect, as described in this policy, aligns with our declarations in the Google Play Data Safety section and the Apple App Privacy labels. If you have questions about how our declarations map to this policy, please contact us.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. For material changes that affect how we collect, use, or share your personal information, we will notify you by email or in-app notification at least 14 days before the changes take effect. For material changes, we will seek your active consent before continuing to process your data under the updated terms.

Non-material changes (such as clarifications or formatting updates) take effect when posted. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent revision.
